“Footsteps of Clarity” is a digital photo essay that follows a silhouette walking through foggy natural landscapes. The photos are processed using custom WebGL shaders.

At first, the images are super blurry and distorted, just like how you feel when you’re anxious. But as the sequence goes on (like taking steps), the code shaders fix the blur, and you can see a clear focus on the green trees and the person’s path.

This series uses visuals to show how walking, doing things that stimulate both sides of your brain, and being in nature can help you feel clearer and get rid of self-criticism.
