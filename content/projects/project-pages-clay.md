Inspired by the traditional Japanese practice of Kintsugi—mending broken pottery with golden lacquer—**Stanzas of Clay** is a series of open ceramic book sculptures. Etched into the unfired clay are raw, scratchy diary entries and freeform poems detailing the dread of being an imposter.

The clay books are intentionally fractured during the firing process, and the resulting cracks are meticulously filled with gold leaf. This visual metaphor highlights that our vulnerabilities, anxieties, and mistakes are not things to be hidden or erased. Instead, they are unique, golden seams of our history that make our creative voices more authentic.
