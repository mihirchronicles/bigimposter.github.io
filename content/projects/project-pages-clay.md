Inspired by the Japanese art of Kintsugi (fixing broken pottery with gold), **Pages of Clay** is a ceramic open book sculpture. The pages are etched with raw, hand-scratched diary entries that reveal feelings of fraudulence.

The ceramic pages are intentionally broken and cracked, but the cracks are filled with bright gold leaf. This symbolizes that our weaknesses and failures are not things to hide, but unique parts of our past that make us stronger and more beautiful.

The sculpture stands as a physical reminder that imperfection and self-compassion are beautiful things.
