Perfectionism is the imposter’s secret weapon. It convinces you that if your project isn’t perfect, you shouldn’t show it to anyone. It tells you: *’Keep polishing, keep editing, keep waiting.’* This is a trap designed to keep you from ever launching.

The builder’s creed is simple: **Shipped is better than perfect.**

When you launch a project with a few bugs or imperfect styling, you prove to yourself that the world doesn’t end when you show flaws. In fact, you get real feedback, make changes, and improve. The feedback loop of building, shipping, getting critiques, and adapting is the fastest way to build real skills.

Skills build confidence. Real confidence isn’t about never making mistakes; it’s about knowing how to fix mistakes when they happen. Stop planning your perfect masterpiece in secret. Build something small, ship it today, and let the real world show you what you’re worth.
