description: "Why launching small, flawed projects beats planning giant, unreleased masterpieces every single time."

---

Perfectionism is the imposter's favorite shield. It convinces you that if your project isn't flawless, you shouldn't show it to the world. It tells you: *'Keep polishing, keep editing, keep waiting.'* This is a trap designed to keep you from ever launching.

The builder's creed is simple: **Shipped is better than perfect.**

When you launch a project that has minor bugs or imperfect styling, you prove to yourself that the world does not collapse when you display flaws. In fact, you receive real feedback, iterate, and improve. The feedback loop of building, shipping, receiving critiques, and adapting is the fastest way to build genuine competence.

Competence breeds confidence. Real confidence is not the belief that you will never make a mistake; it is the knowledge that you can fix mistakes when they happen. Stop planning your perfect masterpiece in secret. Build something small, ship it today, and let the real world guide your worth.
