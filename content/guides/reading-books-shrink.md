Feeling like an imposter? Here are five books that’ll help you shrink that big imposter down to size.

- **’Mindset: The New Psychology of Success’ by Carol S. Dweck:** This book introduces the concept of a Growth Mindset, which means you believe your abilities can be developed. It teaches you to see failure as an opportunity, not as a sign of your identity.
- **’The Gifts of Imperfection’ by Brené Brown:** Brené Brown is a master of letting go of who you think you’re supposed to be and embracing who you actually are. This book is a great way to learn to love yourself flaws and all.
- **’Imposter Syndrome: Mutual Healing’ by Dr. Valerie Young:** This book is the definitive guide to categorizing and tackling the five sub-types of imposters: the Perfectionist, the Superwoman/man, the Natural Genius, the Soloist, and the Expert.
- **’Quiet: The Power of Introverts’ by Susan Cain:** If you feel like an imposter because you don’t fit the loud, extroverted ideal of success, this book is for you. Susan Cain validates introverts and shows you how to build a healthy relationship with your artistic self-doubt.
- **’Big Magic’ by Elizabeth Gilbert:** This book reclaims creativity from fear. It shows you how to build a healthy relationship with your artistic self-doubt and embrace your creativity.

These books work by providing you with a structured way to understand your emotional states. By reading them, you’ll gain the vocabulary to describe your doubt, which is the first step towards conquering it. You’ll realize that your brain is simply playing evolutionary tricks on you to keep you safe from social risk.
