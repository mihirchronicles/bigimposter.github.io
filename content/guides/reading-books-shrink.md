When the inner critic grows loud, reading these five collections can help quiet the mind and rebuild your sense of worth:

- **'Devotions' by Mary Oliver:** Oliver's poems focus on nature and the simple, quiet value of existence. She reminds us that we do not have to be good; we only have to let the soft animal of our body love what it loves.
- **'And Still I Rise' by Maya Angelou:** A collection of fierce self-determination. Angelou's rhythmic, powerful verses serve as a shield against criticism and self-doubt.
- **'The Selected Poetry of Rainer Maria Rilke':** Rilke's famous advice on embracing questions and learning to love the doubts themselves is a balm for any creator who feels lost.
- **'Teaching My Mother How to Give Birth' by Warsan Shire:** Raw and emotional, Shire's poetry validates the pain of not belonging, reminding us that our scars hold creative power.
- **'A Thousand Mornings' by Mary Oliver:** Another beautiful volume focusing on the fresh start of each day, perfect for letting go of yesterday's creative mistakes.

Reading these works provides a vocabulary for your complex emotions, showing you that self-doubt is simply a common landscape that many great minds have traveled.
