The big imposter is a master of spoken language. It whispers vague, heavy threats like: *'You're going to get fired,'* or *'They will eventually figure out you don't know what you are doing.'* Because these thoughts remain abstract, they feel massive and true. Writing them down changes the game entirely.

**Freewriting** is the practice of writing continuously without editing, censoring, or pausing. By setting a timer for 10 minutes and writing down every doubt you have, you drag the imposter out of the shadows and onto physical paper. Once written down, abstract threats become specific, concrete statements.

Look at your written doubts. Under scrutiny, statements like *'I don't know anything'* dissolve. You can ask yourself: *'Is this actually true? Did I not build the feature last week? Did I not coordinate the presentation?'* Paper acts as an objective mirror, proving that the imposter's arguments are illogical exaggerations.

**Action step:** Keep a 'Doubt Journal'. Whenever you feel like a fraud, write down the exact dialogue the imposter is saying. Read it back in a silly voice to strip away its power.
