title: "Give the Critic a Face"
description: "A short poem on making a small, silly object out of your inner critic."
readTime: "2 min read"
---

Your doubt has never had a shape,\
it floats as shadow, vague and wide.\
So draw it small, give it escape\
from the corners where it hides.

Maybe it's a deflated balloon,\
a bird beneath a heavy crown,\
a grumpy, round, cartoon buffoon\
with too many eyes, looking down.

Cut it, fold it, glue it, bend it,\
make it something you can hold.\
A little thing you can pretend\
isn't quite as big as it's been told.

Now it sits there on your desk,\
small and silly, made by you.\
The voice that felt like such a test\
is just a shape — and shapes can move.
