title: "Something You Can Taste"
description: "A short poem on cooking as a small, tangible act of care and proof of effort."
readTime: "2 min read"
---

Chop the onion, stir the pot,\
follow steps someone wrote with care.\
For an hour, you don't need a lot —\
just hands, and heat, and being there.

If it's not exactly right,\
a little salt, a little more —\
no one grades you on this night,\
the only judge is at your door.

Something real comes from this work,\
something warm you get to share.\
No résumé, no hidden quirk,\
just proof that you showed up, and care.

Sit down now and eat what's made,\
let the taste be its own applause.\
You built this meal, course by course laid,\
small, and whole, and worth the pause.
