title: "Rearrange What's Already There"
description: "A short poem on collaging old scraps into something new, with care."
readTime: "2 min read"
---

Take old magazines, old letters,\
torn-up paper, ticket stubs.\
You don't need to be your betters,\
just scissors, glue, and what there was.

Cut a shape from something printed,\
move it somewhere it didn't belong.\
Nothing here was ever meant\
to fit together — but make it strong.

Glue it down. Let the pieces\
find a new and stranger sense.\
Every edge that overlaps releases\
something fixed, something tense.

You took the scraps that were around\
and built something that wasn't there.\
Everything worth finding is found\
in pieces — rearranged with care.
