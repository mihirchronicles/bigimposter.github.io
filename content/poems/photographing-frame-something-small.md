description: "A short poem on photographing small, ordinary details as a quiet act of attention."

Pick up the camera, or your phone,\
and look for one small thing today —\
the light on brick, a leaf alone,\
a shadow stretching across the way.

You don't need a grand subject,\
no skyline, no dramatic scene.\
Just notice what you might reject\
on any other day, unseen.

Frame it. Take the shot. That's all.\
No filter needed, no big claim.\
You looked at something ordinary,\
and found it worthy of a frame.

This is the practice: paying attention,\
not performance, not a show.\
And noticing is its own dimension\
of work that matters. Now you know.
