title: "Small and Whole"
description: "A short poem on building small, finished things instead of one perfect thing."
readTime: "2 min read"
---

Don't try to build the whole today,\
the towering, finished, perfect thing.\
Just lay one brick, just find one way\
to make one small completion ring.

The doubt that says you can't, won't, shouldn't\
grows loud when tasks feel endless, vague.\
But finished things, even the smallest,\
are proof that you don't have to fake.

Five lines, one shelf, one working part,\
something whole, however plain.\
Hold it up — that's a start,\
that's not nothing, that's a gain.

Stack enough small, finished things\
and one day you will turn around\
to see the structure that it brings,\
built from doubt, but standing, sound.
